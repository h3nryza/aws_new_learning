Top Study Areas (Catching Up from 2021)

Priority study list for someone with SAP-C01 knowledge from 2021:
  1. Multi-account governance — Organizations RCPs, Declarative Policies, Control Tower proactive controls, delegated admin pattern
  2. VPC Lattice and Cloud WAN — application-layer service networking and global WAN management
  3. Aurora Serverless v2 and I/O-Optimized — fine-grained scaling replaces v1, new storage pricing tier
  4. S3 Express One Zone — directory buckets with single-digit-millisecond latency for compute-adjacent workloads

  6. Container orchestration — ECS Service Connect for service mesh, EKS Pod Identity replacing IRSA
EXAM TIP: SAP-C02 questions are long, scenario-based, and often have two seemingly correct answers. The key differentiator is usually cost-effectiveness vs operational overhead. "Least operational overhead" is the most common qualifier. Focus on understanding when to use each service, not just what it does.

5. Security, Compliance & Governance for AI 14% Data privacy, model governance, Bedrock Guardrails, content filtering, PII handling, compliance

Key Topics in Detail

Prompt Engineering Techniques

  • Zero-shot: Prompt with no examples — relies on the model's pre-trained knowledge
  • Few-shot: Provide 2–5 examples in the prompt to guide the model's response format and style
  • Chain-of-thought: Ask the model to "think step by step" to improve reasoning accuracy
  • System prompts: Define the model's persona, constraints, and behavioral guidelines

Model Customization Techniques

  • RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): Augment model responses with external knowledge from a vector database at inference time — no model retraining needed
  • Fine-tuning: Further train a pre-trained model on domain-specific labeled data — modifies model weights
  • RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback): Human evaluators rank model outputs to train a reward model that guides further fine-tuning
  • Transfer learning: Apply a model trained on one task to a related but different task
  • Continued pre-training: Extend pre-training with domain-specific unlabeled data (e.g., medical literature)

Evaluation Metrics

  • ROUGE: Recall-Oriented Understudy for Gisting Evaluation — measures overlap between generated and reference summaries
  • BLEU: Bilingual Evaluation Understudy — measures precision of n-gram overlap, commonly used for translation
  • BERTScore: Uses BERT embeddings to compute semantic similarity between generated and reference text
  • Perplexity: Measures how well a probability model predicts a sample — lower is better
  • Human evaluation: Subject matter experts rate relevance, coherence, and helpfulness

Key AWS Services

  • Amazon Bedrock — managed access to foundation models (Claude, Llama, Titan, etc.) with Knowledge Bases, Agents, and Guardrails
  • Amazon SageMaker — build, train, and deploy custom ML models
  • Amazon Q — AI assistant for developers (Q Developer) and business users (Q Business)
  • Amazon Comprehend — NLP: sentiment analysis, entity recognition, language detection
  • Amazon Transcribe — speech to text
  • Amazon Lex — conversational interfaces (chatbots)
  • Amazon Polly — text to speech
  • Amazon Rekognition — image and video analysis
  • Amazon Textract — document text and data extraction (OCR+)

8 Responsible AI Dimensions

  1. Fairness — equitable treatment across groups, bias mitigation
  2. Explainability — ability to understand and interpret model decisions
  3. Robustness — consistent performance across conditions and adversarial inputs
  4. Privacy — protection of personal and sensitive data
  5. Safety — preventing harmful, toxic, or dangerous outputs
  6. Controllability — mechanisms to steer and constrain model behavior
  7. Veracity & Truthfulness — generating accurate, factual, non-hallucinated responses
  8. Governance — oversight, accountability, auditing, and compliance
EXAM TIP: Despite being a foundational exam, AIF-C01 covers a broad range of AI/ML concepts. Focus on understanding RAG vs fine-tuning trade-offs, when to use each prompt engineering technique, and the 8 responsible AI dimensions. Bedrock Guardrails is the AWS-specific responsible AI service to know.

6. Exam Delivery & Recertification

Recertification Policy

Method How It Works Extension Notes
Exam Retake Retake the current version of the exam 3-year extension from the date you pass AWS provides a 50% discount voucher for recertification
Certification Maintenance Complete activities on AWS Skill Builder (digital courses, assessments) 1-year extension Requires an active Skill Builder subscription or use of free maintenance content
Game-Based Renewal Complete AWS Cloud Quest scenarios Renews Cloud Practitioner and AI Practitioner Only available for foundational-level certifications
Cross-Certification Passing a higher-level exam recertifies lower exams 3 years (tied to higher exam validity) Example: DevOps Pro (DOP-C02) recertifies CloudOps Engineer Associate (SOA-C03)
Recertification timeline: Certifications are valid for 3 years. You can begin recertification activities starting 1 year before expiration. If your certification lapses, you must retake the full exam — there is no grace period for maintenance.

7. Key Trends

AWS Certification Trends (2021–2026)

# Trend Details
1 AI/ML is the biggest growth area Three new exams since 2024: AIF-C01 (AI Practitioner), DEA-C01 (Data Engineer), MLA-C01 (ML Engineer). Bedrock, SageMaker, and Amazon Q now appear on nearly every exam. GenAI security added to SCS-C03.
2 Specialty exams are consolidating Database Specialty (DBS-C01) — retired. Machine Learning Specialty (MLS-C01) — retired March 2026, replaced by MLA-C01. Advanced Networking (ANS-C01) — retiring August 2026. Security Specialty is the sole remaining specialty, freshly updated.
3 Security emphasis increasing across all exams Security domains have increased weight on SAA-C03 (30%), DVA-C02 (26%), and DOP-C02 (17% as a standalone domain). IAM Identity Center, Organizations policies, and encryption are universal topics.
4 Container/serverless now standard on nearly every exam ECS, EKS, Fargate, and Lambda are no longer "advanced" topics. Container deployment patterns, service mesh (Service Connect), and serverless architectures are expected knowledge at all levels above Foundational.
5 IaC diversification CDK v2 is now as important as CloudFormation on DOP-C02 and SAP-C02. SAM remains relevant for serverless. Understanding construct levels (L1/L2/L3), CDK Pipelines, and cdk synth/cdk deploy is required.
6 New question formats require deeper understanding Ordering and Matching questions (on AIF-C01, MLA-C01, SCS-C03, SOA-C03) test relationships and sequences, not just recognition. Process-of-elimination strategies are less effective. Hands-on experience is increasingly valuable.
Overall direction: AWS is moving toward fewer, broader exams at the Associate level (consolidating specialties downward) while increasing the depth and breadth of Professional exams. AI/ML content is being woven into every certification path. The days of memorizing service names and basic features are fading — scenario-based questions with subtle trade-offs are the norm.
STUDY STRATEGY: For someone catching up from 2021, prioritize in this order: (1) Professional exam updates (SAP-C02, DOP-C02) since these are likely your recertification targets. (2) Security Specialty (SCS-C03) if security is part of your role. (3) AI/ML fundamentals — even if you do not plan to take AIF-C01 or MLA-C01, GenAI topics now appear on Professional exams. (4) Associate exams only as reference material for foundational concepts.
